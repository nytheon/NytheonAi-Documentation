About Large Language Models (LLMs)

LLMs are trained on vast data to understand and generate human language. Nytheon's transformer-based LLM handles summarization, translation, reasoning, coding, and content generation across long contexts with adaptive memory.

Training Methodology

Nytheon's LLM was trained on a diverse corpus of text from scientific papers, code repositories, literature, and web content using a novel training approach that combines:

  • Supervised fine-tuning on specific tasks
  • Reinforcement learning from human feedback
  • Multi-task learning across domains
  • Continual learning to adapt to new information
  • Uncensored training for unbiased responses

What is a Neural Network?

Neural networks are systems of artificial neurons that mimic human brain structures. Nytheon utilizes a hybrid stack of CNNs, RNNs, and transformers for vision, language, and cross-modal intelligence.

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

Specialized for processing grid-like data such as images. Used in Nytheon's vision module for object detection and image classification.

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)

Designed for sequential data. Used in Nytheon for time-series analysis and speech processing.

Transformers

State-of-the-art architecture for handling sequences with attention mechanisms. Foundation of Nytheon's language models.
